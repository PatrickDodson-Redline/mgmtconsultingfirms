The same growth leader's week, with and without Redline Growth.

Dana heads a 15 to 30 person office, carrying client work and the firm's growth at the same time. Here is the same week, twice. The five beats track the five phases of the pursuit cycle.

The week, in five beatsWithout Redline GrowthWith Redline Growth
Beat 1Be present and get known The means to publish and reach top accounts. Client work eats the week. It never happens. Outreach and posts go out in growth leaders' voices, on cadence. The warm path is mapped before any RFP.
Beat 2See who is moving Hears about a target's new strategy hire from the same alert everyone gets. A competitor is already in for coffee. The signal is caught as it forms, with a dossier and the right IP ready to share.
Beat 3Spend growth leader time where it converts The pursuit list is whatever walked in. Two growth leaders burn a month on a deal they were never winning. A monthly brief scores the accounts worth the time. No hours on the long shots.
Beat 4Show up in the know You or your team scramble to piece together info when an opportunity pops up. What you find is spotty or possibly stale. Every live pursuit triggers a dossier: context, buying committee, the opening, the IP to cite.
Beat 5Grow and keep the book Expansion moves only when two growth leaders share a hallway. An account is quietly shopping, and they learn when the work stops. The book is monitored for risk and for cross-practice openings, surfaced early, and informed by your first-party data and patterns.
